A solution is prepared by dissolving 25 kg of sugar in 100 kg of water. Calculate the mass percentage of the solute.

```markdown
Mass percentage of solute:
$\% = \frac{\text{Mass of solute}}{\text{Total mass}} \times 100$

Given:
Mass of solute = 25 \text{ kg}  
Mass of solvent = 100 \text{ kg}  

Total mass:
= 25 + 100  
= 125 \text{ kg}

Substituting:
$\% = \frac{25}{125} \times 100$

$\% = 20\%$

Therefore:
Mass percentage of solute = 20\%
```
