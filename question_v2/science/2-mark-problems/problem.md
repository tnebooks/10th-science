If the hydroxide ion concentration in a solution is $1.0 \times 10^{-9}$ mole, what is its pOH value?

```markdown
pOH is the negative logarithm of the hydroxide ion concentration.

Given:
$[OH^-] = 1.0 \times 10^{-9}$

Formula:
$pOH = -\log_{10}[OH^-]$

Substituting:
$pOH = -\log_{10}(1 \times 10^{-9})$

$\log_{10}(10^{-9}) = -9$

Therefore:
$pOH = -(-9) = 9$
```
