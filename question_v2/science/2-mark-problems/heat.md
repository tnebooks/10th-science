A resistor of $5\ \Omega$ carries a current of 6 A. Calculate the heat produced in 5 minutes.

```markdown
Joule's law:
$H = I^2 R t$

Given:
$R = 5\ \Omega$
$I = 6 \text{ A}$
$t = 5 \text{ min} = 300 \text{ s}$

Substituting:
$H = 6^2 \times 5 \times 300$

$H = 36 \times 5 \times 300$

$H = 54000 \text{ J}$

Therefore:
Heat produced, $H = 54000 \text{ J}$
```
