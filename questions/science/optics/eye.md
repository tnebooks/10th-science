In a myopic eye, the image of the object is formed:


```markdown
  In a myopic (near-sighted) eye, the eyeball is either too long or the lens is
  too curved. This causes light rays from a distant object to converge and form
  an image in front of the retina instead of directly on it, resulting in
  blurred distance vision.
```
