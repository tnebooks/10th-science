```markdown

  The refractive index ($\mu$) is defined as the ratio of the speed of light in a vacuum to the speed of light in a specific medium. The formula is:$$\mu = \frac{c}{v}$$Where:$c$ is the constant speed of light in a vacuum (approximately $3 \times 10^8$ m/s).$v$ is the velocity of light in the medium.From this formula, we can see that $\mu \propto 1/v$. This means the velocity of light in a medium is inversely proportional to the refractive index of that medium.
```
