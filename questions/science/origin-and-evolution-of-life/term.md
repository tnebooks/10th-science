The term Ethnobotany was coined by:


```markdown
  The term 'Ethnobotany' was first coined by the American botanist John William
  Harshberger in 1895. He defined it as the study of the plants used by
  primitive and aboriginal people. This field examines the relationship between
  humans and plants, including their use for food, medicine, and rituals.
```
