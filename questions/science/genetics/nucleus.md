answers:
  - secondary constriction
explanation: >-
  The nucleolus is formed at a specific location on certain chromosomes 
  called the secondary constriction, also known as the Nucleolar Organizer 
  Region (NOR). While the primary constriction (centromere) is present in 
  all chromosomes for spindle attachment, only specific chromosomes have 
  the secondary constriction responsible for producing ribosomal RNA and 
  forming the nucleolus.
