answers:
  - Centromere
explanation: >-
  The centromere is the specialized region of a chromosome where the two sister
  chromatids are held together and where the kinetochore forms. During cell
  division (mitosis or meiosis), spindle fibers attach to the kinetochore at the
  centromere to pull the chromatids apart toward opposite poles of the cell.
