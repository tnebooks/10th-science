The centromere is found at the centre of the chromosome.


```markdown
  Chromosomes are classified based on the position of the centromere. In a
  metacentric chromosome, the centromere is located exactly at the center,
  resulting in two arms of equal length (forming a 'V' shape during anaphase).
  In sub-metacentric, it is slightly off-center; in acrocentric, it is near one
  end; and in telocentric, it is at the very tip.
```
