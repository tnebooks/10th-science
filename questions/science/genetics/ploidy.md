  - "Aneuploidy"
  - "Euploidy"
  - "polyploidy"
answer:
  - "Aneuploidy"
explanation: "Aneuploidy is a condition where the chromosome number is not an exact multiple of the haploid set. It involves the loss (monosomy, nullisomy) or gain (trisomy, tetrasomy) of one or more individual chromosomes. This differs from euploidy/polyploidy, where entire sets of chromosomes are added or lost."
