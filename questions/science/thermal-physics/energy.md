answer:
  - "difference in T.E and P.E"
explanation: "Total Energy (T.E) of a substance is the sum of its Kinetic Energy (K.E) and Potential Energy (P.E). Temperature is specifically a measure of the average Kinetic Energy of the molecules. Mathematically, $K.E = T.E - P.E$. Therefore, temperature represents the average difference between the Total Energy and the Potential Energy."
