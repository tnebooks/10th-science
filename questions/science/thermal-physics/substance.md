 If a substance is heated or cooled, the change in mass of that substance is:

```markdown
According to the Law of Conservation of Mass, mass can neither be created nor destroyed in a physical or chemical change. When a substance is heated or cooled, its temperature and volume may change (causing a change in density), but the total quantity of matter—and thus its mass—remains constant (zero change).
```
