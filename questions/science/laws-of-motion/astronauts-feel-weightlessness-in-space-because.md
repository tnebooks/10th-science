Astronauts feel weightlessness in space because------?

```markdown
Astronauts feel weightlessness in space because **they are under free fall condition**. 

* **The Misconception:** A common misconception is that there is no gravity in space. In reality, at the altitude of the International Space Station (about 400 km above Earth), gravity is still about 90% as strong as it is on the ground.
* **The Reality of Free Fall:** The space station and the astronauts inside it are traveling at a very high forward speed (orbital velocity). As gravity pulls them down toward Earth, they move forward so fast that the Earth curves away beneath them. Consequently, they are continuously falling *around* the Earth.
* **Zero Apparent Weight:** Because the astronauts and their spacecraft are falling together at the exact same rate under gravity, there is no surface pushing back up against the astronauts. Without a reaction force ($R = 0$), they experience a state of continuous free fall, which feels exactly like having no weight.
```
