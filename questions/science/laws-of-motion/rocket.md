  - "Newton's law of gravitation"
  - "law of conservation of linear momentum"
  - "Newton's first law of motion"
answer:
  - "Newton's third law of motion"
explanation: "Rocket propulsion is primarily based on Newton's Third Law of Motion (action-reaction principle). The rocket expels hot gases backward at high velocity (action), and the reaction force pushes the rocket forward. While conservation of linear momentum is also involved in the overall system, the fundamental principle that explains how rockets move is the action-reaction pair. Newton's law of gravitation explains planetary motion and attraction, not rocket propulsion. Newton's first law (inertia) describes motion in absence of forces, not propulsion mechanisms."
