A body will be in equilibrium, if the resultant force of all the forces acting on the body is equal to-----?

```markdown
For a body to be in a state of **equilibrium**, it must not experience any change in its state of rest or uniform motion. According to Newton's laws of motion, an acceleration can only be produced when a net **unbalanced force** acts on an object. 

When multiple forces act on a body simultaneously, their combined effect is represented by a single vector sum called the **resultant force**. 

* **If the resultant force is equal to zero:** The forces perfectly cancel each other out (**balanced forces**). The body experiences no net acceleration and remains in equilibrium.
* **If the resultant force is not equal to zero:** The forces are **unbalanced**, causing the body to speed up, slow down, or change its direction.

Therefore, a body will remain in equilibrium only if the net resultant force acting on it is exactly **zero**.
```
