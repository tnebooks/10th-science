If a person whose mass is 60kg stands on the surface of Earth, his weight would be 588 N his weight in moon is -----?

```markdown
The correct answer is **$97.5 \, \text{N}$**.

### Mathematical Calculation:

Weight ($W$) is defined as the gravitational force exerted on a body. It depends on the mass ($m$) of the body and the acceleration due to gravity ($g$) of the celestial body it stands on.

**Formula:**
$$W = m \times g$$



**Given Data:**
* Mass of the person ($m$) = $60 \, \text{kg}$ (Mass remains constant everywhere in the universe).
* Acceleration due to gravity on the Moon ($g_{\text{moon}}$) = $1.625 \, \text{m} \, \text{s}^{-2}$ (which is about $0.165$ times or $\frac{1}{6}\text{th}$ of Earth's gravity).

**Substitution:**
$$W_{\text{moon}} = 60 \times 1.625$$
$$W_{\text{moon}} = 97.5 \, \text{N}$$

For comparison, on Earth, the weight is $60 \times 9.8 = 588 \, \text{N}$. On the Moon, because gravity is much weaker, the weight drops significantly to $97.5 \, \text{N}$ even though the person's actual mass is still exactly $60 \, \text{kg}$.
```
