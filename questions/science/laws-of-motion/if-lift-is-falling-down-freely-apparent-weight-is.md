If lift is falling down freely, apparent weight is equal to-------?

```markdown
The correct answer is **Zero**.

### Concept Explanation:

Apparent weight is the weight experienced by a person or object inside a lift. It is equal to the normal reaction force exerted by the floor of the lift.

When a lift falls freely under gravity, both the lift and the person inside it accelerate downward with the same acceleration due to gravity ($g$). Since there is no supporting force from the floor, the normal reaction force becomes zero.

Therefore, the apparent weight of the person is zero. This condition is called **weightlessness**.

### Formula:

$$
W_{\text{apparent}} = m(g - a)
$$

### Given Data / Calculation:

* Mass of the person = $m$
* Acceleration due to gravity = $g$
* Acceleration of the freely falling lift = $a = g$

**Substitution:**

$$
W_{\text{apparent}} = m(g - g)
$$

$$
W_{\text{apparent}} = m(0)
$$

$$
W_{\text{apparent}} = 0
$$

### Scientific Conclusion:

When a lift falls freely, both the lift and the person inside it move downward with the same acceleration. As a result, there is no normal reaction force acting on the person, and the apparent weight becomes zero.
```
