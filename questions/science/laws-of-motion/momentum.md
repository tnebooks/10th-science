---
choices:
  - "Impulsive force"
  - "Acceleration"
  - "Force"
  - "Change in velocity"
answer:
  - "Force"
explanation: "The slope of a momentum-time graph is calculated as Δp/Δt (change in momentum divided by change in time). According to Newton's Second Law of Motion, F = dp/dt, which means force equals the rate of change of momentum. Therefore, the slope directly gives the force acting on the object. Impulsive force refers to force acting over a short time interval, acceleration is the rate of change of velocity, and change in velocity is not represented by this slope."
