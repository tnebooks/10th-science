### Scientific Explanation:

According to Newton's Second Law of Motion, force is equal to the rate of change of momentum. This relationship is quantified by the concept of **Impulse ($J$)**, which is defined as the product of force ($F$) and the time interval ($\Delta t$) over which it acts:

$$J = F \times \Delta t = \Delta p$$

Where $\Delta p$ represents the change in momentum. Because it is a multiplication product, you can achieve the exact same numerical change in momentum ($\Delta p$) in two distinct ways:

1. **High Force, Short Time:** Applying a massive amount of force instantly (e.g., a bat hitting a cricket ball, or an airbag inflating during a car crash).
2. **Low Force, Long Time:** Applying a gentle, prolonged force over a longer duration (e.g., a car smoothly braking over several seconds, or a fielder pulling their hands back to catch a ball).
