```markdown
A system can be brought to equilibrium by applying a force which is **equal in magnitude but opposite direction as that of resultant force**. This specific balancing force is called the **'Equilibrant'**.

* **Resultant Force:** When multiple forces act on a body, their combined vector sum is represented by a single net force called the resultant force, which causes the body to accelerate.
* **Achieving Equilibrium:** To stop this acceleration and bring the system to rest or equilibrium, the total net force must become zero. This can only be achieved by applying an identical force (same magnitude) directly against the path of motion (opposite direction) to completely cancel it out.
```
