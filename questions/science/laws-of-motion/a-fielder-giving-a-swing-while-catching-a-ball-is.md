A fielder giving a swing while catching a ball is an example of ------?

```markdown
A fielder pulling their hands backward while catching a ball is a classic example of **Newton’s II law of motion**.

According to Newton's second law and the concept of Impulse:

$$F = \frac{\Delta p}{t}$$

Where $F$ represents the force experienced, $\Delta p$ is the change in linear momentum, and $t$ is the time interval.

* **Increasing the Time ($t$):** By riding the catch and swinging the hands backward, the fielder increases the total **time interval ($t$)** taken to bring the high-velocity ball's momentum to a complete stop.
* **Reducing the Force ($F$):** As seen in the equation, force is inversely proportional to time. By extending the duration of the impact, the **damaging force ($F$)** exerted by the ball onto the fielder's palms is drastically reduced.

If the fielder kept their hands completely still, the time interval ($t$) would be near zero, resulting in an incredibly large, instantaneous force that could cause severe injury or cause the ball to bounce right out of their grip.
```
