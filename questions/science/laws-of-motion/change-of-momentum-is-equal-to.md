Change of momentum is equal to -----?

```markdown
The change of momentum is equal to **Impulse**.

### Scientific Explanation:

This relationship is derived directly from Newton's Second Law of Motion, which states that the force ($F$) acting on a body is equal to the rate of change of its linear momentum ($\Delta p$) over time ($t$).

$$F = \frac{\Delta p}{t}$$

If we rearrange this equation by multiplying both sides by time ($t$), we get:

$$F \times t = \Delta p$$

By definition, the product of an impulsive force and the short time interval for which it acts is known as **Impulse ($J$)**:

$$J = F \times t$$

Therefore, combining these two concepts proves that:

$$J = \Delta p$$

Hence, **Impulse = Change in momentum**. Its SI unit is $\text{kg} \, \text{m} \, \text{s}^{-1}$ or $\text{N} \, \text{s}$.
```
