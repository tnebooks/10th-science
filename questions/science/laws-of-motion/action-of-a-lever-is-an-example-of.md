Action of a lever is an example of ------?

```markdown
The action of a lever is an example of an **Unbalanced force** (which creates a turning effect or torque that causes motion).

* **Mechanism of a Lever:** A lever consists of a rigid bar that rotates around a fixed pivot point called a fulcrum. When you apply an effort force at one end to lift a load at the other end, the system is driven by **unbalanced forces** that create an unequal clockwise or counterclockwise moment. 
* **Why Unbalanced?** If the forces and moments on both sides of the fulcrum were perfectly equal, the lever would be a *balanced force* system and remain completely still (in equilibrium). For the lever to actually move, tilt, or lift a load, the net torque must be non-zero, meaning the forces acting on the system must be unbalanced.
```
