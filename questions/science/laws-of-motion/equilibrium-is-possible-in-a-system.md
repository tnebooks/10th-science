```markdown
Equilibrium is possible in a **Closed** "system."

### Scientific Explanation:

In physics and thermodynamics, systems are classified based on whether they exchange matter and energy with their surroundings:

* **Open System:** This system can freely exchange both energy (heat) and matter (particles) with the outside environment. Because new matter or external disruptions can constantly enter or leave, achieving a permanent, stable state of equilibrium is impossible.
* **Closed System:** This system can exchange energy (heat and work) with its surroundings, but **cannot exchange matter**. Because the total amount of matter inside remains completely constant and isolated from external mass transfers, the forces and states within the system can eventually balance out perfectly, making **equilibrium** possible.
* **Isolated System:** This system can exchange neither energy nor matter with its surroundings, and equilibrium is easily achieved here as well.

Therefore, for static or dynamic equilibrium to be maintained without constant external mass disruptions, the system must be closed (or isolated).
```
