  - Spontaneous radioactivity
answers:
  - Induced radioactivity
  - Artificial radioactivity
explanation: >-
  When a stable nucleus is bombarded with high-energy particles (such as
  neutrons, protons, or alpha particles), it becomes unstable and undergoes
  radioactive decay. This process is called induced radioactivity or artificial
  radioactivity. It was first discovered by Irène Curie and Frédéric Joliot in
  1934 when they bombarded aluminum with alpha particles to produce radioactive
  phosphorus.
