  - "Kalpakkam"
explanation: "India's first nuclear reactor, Apsara (1 MW, swimming pool type research reactor), was commissioned on August 4, 1956 at the Bhabha Atomic Research Centre (BARC) in Mumbai (then Bombay). However, India's first indigenous nuclear power plant was the Madras Atomic Power Station (MAPS) at Kalpakkam, Tamil Nadu, commissioned in 1984. If referring to the first research reactor, the answer is Mumbai; if referring to the first power plant, it is Kalpakkam. Based on standard Tamil Nadu State Board physics curriculum context where this question commonly appears, Kalpakkam is the expected answer as the first nuclear power station."
