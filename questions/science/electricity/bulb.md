In a simple circuit, why does the bulb glow when you close the switch?

```markdown
  For an electric current to flow, there must be a continuous, unbroken path 
  from the power source to the device and back. A closed switch provides 
  this continuous path, allowing electrons to move through the bulb and 
  light it up.
```
