An electric heater of resistance 5 Ω is connected to an electric source. If a current of 6 A
flows through the heater, then find the amount of heat produced in 5 minutes.

```markdown
  The heat produced ($H$) is given by Joule's Law of Heating: $H = I^2Rt$.
  Given: Current $I = 6\text{ A}$, Resistance $R = 5\ \Omega$, and 
  Time $t = 5\text{ minutes} = 300\text{ seconds}$.
  Calculation: $H = 6^2 \times 5 \times 300 = 36 \times 5 \times 300 = 180 \times 300 = 54000\text{ J}$.
  *Correction: Based on the calculation $36 \times 1500$, the result is 54000J.*
```
