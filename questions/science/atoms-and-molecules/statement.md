  - 12 gram of C-12 contains Avogadro's number of atoms.
  - One mole of oxygen gas contains Avogadro's number of molecules.
  - One mole of electrons stands for 6.023 × 10²³ electrons.
answers:
  - One mole of hydrogen gas contains Avogadro's number of atoms.
explanation: "Hydrogen gas exists naturally as a diatomic molecule ($H_2$). Therefore, one mole of hydrogen gas contains Avogadro's number ($6.023 \times 10^{23}$) of molecules, but since each molecule has two atoms, it actually contains $2 \times 6.023 \times 10^{23}$ atoms. The other statements correctly apply the definition of a mole to atoms, molecules, and electrons respectively."
