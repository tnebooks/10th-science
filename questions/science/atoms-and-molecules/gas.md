answer:
  - "22.4 litre"
explanation: "According to Avogadro's Law, equal volumes of all gases, under the same conditions of temperature and pressure, contain an equal number of molecules. Specifically, at S.T.P. (Standard Temperature and Pressure), 1 mole of any gas—whether it is monoatomic (like He), diatomic (like $H_2$ or $O_2$), or polyatomic (like $CO_2$)—occupies a volume of 22.4 litres. This is known as the Gram Molar Volume."
