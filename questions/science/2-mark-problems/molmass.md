Calculate the molecular mass of the following:

(i) $H_2O$  
(ii) $CO_2$  
(iii) $Ca_3(PO_4)_2$

```markdown
Molecular mass = Sum of atomic masses

(i) $H_2O$:
$H = 1$, $O = 16$

Molecular mass:
$= (1 \times 2) + (16 \times 1)$
$= 2 + 16$
$= 18 \text{ g}$

(ii) $CO_2$:
$C = 12$, $O = 16$

Molecular mass:
$= (12 \times 1) + (16 \times 2)$
$= 12 + 32$
$= 44 \text{ g}$

(iii) $Ca_3(PO_4)_2$:
$Ca = 40$, $P = 30$, $O = 16$

Molecular mass:
$= (40 \times 3) + [(30 + (16 \times 4)) \times 2]$

$= 120 + (94 \times 2)$

$= 120 + 188$

$= 308 \text{ g}$

Therefore:
(i) 18 g  
(ii) 44 g  
(iii) 308 g
```
