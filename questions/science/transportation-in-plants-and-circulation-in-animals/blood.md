---
choices:
  - "O group"
  - "AB group"
  - "A or B group"
  - "all blood group"
answer:
  - "O group"
explanation: "People with blood group O are known as universal donors because their red blood cells lack A and B antigens. However, their plasma contains both anti-A and anti-B antibodies. Therefore, an individual with blood group O can only receive blood from another O group donor; otherwise, their antibodies would attack the A or B antigens in the donor blood, causing a dangerous reaction."
