When a sound wave travels through air, the air particles:


```markdown
  Sound waves are longitudinal waves. In a longitudinal wave, the particles of
  the medium (air) displace or vibrate back and forth parallel to the direction
  that the wave travels. This creates regions of high pressure (compressions)
  and low pressure (rarefactions).
```
