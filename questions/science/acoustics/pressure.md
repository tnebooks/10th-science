  - "165 ms⁻¹"
  - "330 × √2 ms⁻¹"
  - "320/√2 ms⁻¹"
answer:
  - "330 × √2 ms⁻¹"
explanation: "The velocity of sound in a gas is directly proportional to the square root of its absolute temperature ($v \\propto \\sqrt{T}$) and is independent of changes in pressure. If the temperature is doubled ($2T$), the new velocity becomes $v \\times \\sqrt{2}$. Since the initial velocity is 330 ms⁻¹, the new value is $330 \\times \\sqrt{2}$ ms⁻¹, regardless of the pressure being halved."
