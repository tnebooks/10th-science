choices:

  - "-oic acid"
  - "-al"

answer:
  - "-al"
explanation: "In IUPAC nomenclature, aldehydes are named by replacing the terminal '-e' of the corresponding alkane with the suffix '-al'. For example, methane becomes methanal and ethane becomes ethanal. Therefore, the secondary suffix used for an aldehyde is '-al'."
