  - CH≡CH < CH4 < CH2 = CH2
  - CH ≡ CH < CH2 = CH2 < CH4
  - CH4 < CH ≡ CH < CH2 = CH2
answers:
  - CH4 < CH2 = CH2 < CH ≡ CH
explanation: >-
  The reactivity of hydrocarbons depends on the type of carbon-carbon bonds. 
  Alkanes ($CH_4$) are the least reactive because they contain only single 
  $\sigma$ bonds. Alkenes ($CH_2=CH_2$) are more reactive due to the 
  presence of a double bond ($\pi$ bond). Alkynes ($CH \equiv CH$) are 
  generally the most reactive in this group because the triple bond 
  contains two $\pi$ bonds, which are centers of high electron density 
  easily attacked by reagents.
