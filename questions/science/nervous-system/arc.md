  - "receptor, spinal cord, muscle"
explanation: "A reflex arc is the neural pathway that controls a reflex action. It begins at a receptor (which detects the stimulus), sends a signal through a sensory neuron to the spinal cord (the integration center), and then returns a signal via a motor neuron to an effector, such as a muscle. Crucially, most simple reflex arcs bypass the brain to ensure a faster response."
