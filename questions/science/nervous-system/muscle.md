  - "efferent neuron"
explanation: "Efferent neurons, also known as motor neurons, carry nerve impulses away from the central nervous system (brain and spinal cord) toward effectors such as muscles or glands to initiate a response. In contrast, afferent (sensory) neurons carry signals toward the CNS, and association (interneurons) neurons connect the two within the CNS."
