The neurons which carries impulse from the central nervous system to the muscle fibre:


```markdown
  Efferent neurons, also known as motor neurons, carry nerve impulses away from
  the central nervous system (brain and spinal cord) toward effectors such as
  muscles or glands to initiate a response. In contrast, afferent (sensory)
  neurons carry signals toward the CNS, and association (interneurons) neurons
  connect the two within the CNS.
```
